About

I am a lecturer and researcher at the CAPS group of Prof. Martin Schulz at Technische Universität München (TUM). My research focus is energy and power management of HPC systems, and extends to general high performance computing, performance optimization, and computer architecture.

Before joining TUM, I was working at Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität Mümchen (LMU) to accquire my doctoral degree. My doctoral studies include exchanges as visiting researcher to Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory (LLNL) as well as a three-year funded research position at Intel.

Research Topics

  • geopm - Global Extensible Open Power Manager
  • Energy efficient High Performance Computing
  • Scalable Performance Tools
  • Exascale Computing
  • Performance Engineering in HPC
  • Scalability and portable performance
